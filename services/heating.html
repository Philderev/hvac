Safety first, efficiency second, comfort always

A furnace that sat idle through nine months of cooling season deserves a checkup before you trust it with your family's air. Our heating service starts with a combustion analysis and cracked-heat-exchanger inspection — the failure that puts carbon monoxide into living rooms — followed by burner cleaning, ignition testing and airflow verification.

Running a heat pump instead? We verify defrost cycles, reversing-valve operation and auxiliary heat staging so your winter bills stay as small as the season.

Good to know

Heating questions

Does Las Vegas really need heating service?

Yes — valley nights run below 40°F from December through February, and high-desert zones like Summerlin see hard freezes. A furnace failure on a 28° night is a genuine emergency, and we treat it like one.

How often should a furnace be inspected?

Annually, ideally each fall. Gas furnaces get a combustion and CO safety check; heat pumps get a full heating-mode verification. Vega Shield includes this visit automatically.

What are signs my furnace is unsafe?

Yellow or flickering burner flames, soot streaks, a acrid smell on startup, or a CO detector above 9 ppm. Shut the system off and call us — the inspection is free if a repair is needed.
